# About Me

Hey, I'm Pasan — also known online as **IamPasan**.

I'm a network technology and cybersecurity student with a serious focus on offensive security. Not the "watched a few YouTube videos and called myself a hacker" kind — I'm working through a structured path: **eJPT → PNPT → OSCP**, building real, demonstrable skills one box at a time.

## What I'm doing

Most of my time goes into:

- **HTB (Hack The Box)** machines — enumeration, exploitation, privilege escalation, the whole chain
- **CTFs** — sharpening problem-solving under pressure
- **Active Directory lab exercises** — because AD is where real-world engagements actually live

This blog is where I document that process. Think of it less as a highlight reel and more as a working logbook — the recon, the dead ends, the "wait, why didn't that work," and eventually, the root flag.

## What you'll find here

- Full penetration testing writeups (HTB, THM, and lab environments)
- Notes on tools and techniques as I learn them
- Honest documentation — including what *didn't* work, because that's usually the more useful part

## Why I'm doing this publicly

Certifications prove you can pass an exam. A public portfolio proves you can actually think. I'm building this alongside my certification path so there's a real trail of work — not just a PDF — backing up what I know.
